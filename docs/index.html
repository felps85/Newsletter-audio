Proof of Concept

A calmer way to keep up with newsletters.

ListenUp is a local-first macOS prototype that pulls newsletters into one place, strips away the noise, preserves the original layout when you want it, and turns the important content into something you can listen to.

Download macOS prototype Install notes

What we were trying to build

A local inbox for newsletter listening.

The goal was to explore a simple desktop experience where newsletters live outside the clutter of email, can be grouped by source, and can be read either as clean text or in their original newsletter layout.

Why local-first

Private by default and inexpensive to run.

This prototype runs on your Mac, stores data locally, uses macOS-friendly credential storage, and avoids the cost and complexity of maintaining a hosted backend while the product direction is still evolving.

What works today

Local Gmail or IMAP sync

Connect a mailbox locally and sync newsletters into a dedicated reading interface.

Reader and Original modes

Switch between a cleaned reading view and the original newsletter layout when it’s available.

Audio playback

Listen to the important content with local speech playback instead of reading long email threads.

Local desktop prototype

Testing note

Why installing the app still takes an extra step

We are not paying for the Apple Developer Program yet, so this prototype is not signed or notarized by Apple.

That means macOS may warn that it cannot verify the app. The build is meant for proof-of-concept testing, but it does require a manual confirmation to open.

  1. Download the DMG and drag ListenUp.app into Applications.
  2. Right-click the app and choose Open.
  3. If macOS blocks it, go to System Settings → Privacy & Security.
  4. Use Open Anyway for the app, then launch it again.
